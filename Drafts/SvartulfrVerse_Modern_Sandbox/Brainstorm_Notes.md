# Brainstorm Notes — SvartulfrVerse Modern (Slice of Life under Siege)

## The premise that landed
The Douglas-Bloodmoon family maintains a pristine corporate facade (Douglas Commerce Company) in Century City, but behind closed doors, they are a massive illegal arms dealer and operate a private mercenary army (DCC Security). The central tension is a "Slice of Life under Siege": the 19-year-old twins (Jasper and the protagonist/user) are trying to carve out normal lives, go to UCLA, and have underground parties, while constantly trying to evade the suffocating, paranoid overprotection of their billionaire father, Erik. Erik's paranoia is justified because the family's arms dealing puts a constant target on their backs from rival cartels, rogue mercenaries, and betrayed governments.

## Anchors worth carrying
- The contrast between the clean corporate world and the gritty illegal arms trade.
- The "TwinXFamily" dynamic: Jasper and the protagonist acting as partners in crime to bypass family security.
- Erik's helicopter parenting is backed by an actual paramilitary force. Malachia acting as a physical wall, Noah as a legal shield, Logan providing a neutral safe haven.
- UCLA as a battleground for independence versus the heavily monitored Beverly Hills estate.

## Leaning (not decided)
- World Mode: Sandbox. The user explicitly requested an open-ended sandbox to support the "slice of life" feel, with the possibility of triggering character-specific arcs dynamically during play.
- Tone: High-stakes PMC thriller mixed with young-adult slice of life. Like *Succession* meets *Spy x Family* and *Sicario*.

## Directions explored but set aside
- Boardroom Succession War: Focus shifted away from internal family backstabbing for the throne towards a united (if dysfunctional) family dealing with external threats and the twins' desire for freedom.

## Loose fragments
- "Selling weapons to anyone" puts them in the crosshairs of many.
- The Verve (Logan's garage-lounge) as a PMC-free safe zone.
