## 1. CORE CONCEPT & TONE **[REQUIRED]**

**Logline:** A fallen angel disguised as a ruthless drug lord finds cosmic redemption and divine reconciliation through his love for a broken, heroin-addicted mother.

**Genre & Tone:** The story begins as grimdark, visceral survival horror and gritty street-level drama, transitioning into a supernatural romance, and ultimately culminating in a divine, awe-inspiring cosmic epic. During intimate and sexual scenes between Andrei and Anna, the tone shifts to sensory, tasteful erotica — focusing on physical sensation, Anna's body and its responses, touch, warmth, and closeness. When flashbacks of sexual abuse occur (Jack's violence, prostitution), the tone becomes dark and unflinching, treating trauma with painful, grounded realism — never titillating, always the language of survival and damage.

**Emotional Payoff:** A profound sense of healing, awe, and peace. The story moves from the absolute lowest pits of human despair to divine grace and cosmic belonging. Every beat earns its weight. The player should feel that love is not weakness — it is the one force in the universe capable of rewriting destiny.

**Tonal Rules (Hard):**
- Never make violence, addiction, or prostitution feel glamorous or consequence-free. Withdrawal and physical abuse must be rendered with painful, grounded physical realism.
- Never let the supernatural feel mundane. Demonic and angelic interventions must carry genuine physical, psychological, and sensory weight.
- The tone shift from gritty street reality to divine epic must be slow, earned, and anchored in Anna's growth — not in spectacle alone.
- Sexual content between Anna and Andrei is intimacy-first. Trauma flashbacks are never sexualized — they are rendered as the language of damage.

## 2. THE WORLD — Tier 1 Lorebook Material

### 2a. The Setting **[REQUIRED]**

**Physical Location:** Present-day Earth. Primarily the rain-slicked, neon-lit underbelly of Los Angeles — skid row adjacent streets, rundown motels, and the specific kind of poverty that hides behind fast food wrappers and ammonia. Contrasted sharply with the hyper-sterile luxury of Andrei's penthouse high above the city.

**Atmosphere & Sensory Signature:**
- Smell: Street level — cheap bleach, stale cigarette smoke, rain on hot asphalt, the sour ghost of cooked heroin. Penthouse — expensive cologne, sterile cleaning products, and underneath both, a faint persistent note of sulfur and copper that no one discusses.
- Sound: Thrumming city traffic below, rain against glass, silences that arrive too suddenly, violent sounds that end too fast. Later: the deafening, pressure-drop static hum of angelic presence, a sound that bypasses the ears.
- Light/Visual: Sick amber sodium streetlamps, bruised skin under fluorescent motel lighting, sharp penthouse whites and chrome. Later: blinding, shadowless divine light that makes everything else look like a photograph of itself.
- Physical sensation: A bone-deep cold at street level and during demonic proximity — the cold of withdrawal, the cold of something vast and old standing nearby. Slowly thaws into warmth as Anna heals and the penthouse becomes a home.

### 2b. The Rules of Reality **[REQUIRED]**

**Rule 1 — Demonic Disguises:** All demons operating on Earth must maintain a complete human form. The disguise requires continuous will. The sole tell is their eyes — glowing red — which they hide behind dark sunglasses at all times. Under extreme emotional or physical duress, the disguise fractures: the ambient temperature drops sharply, shadows lengthen and move incorrectly, and the red bleeds around the edges of the glasses. Andrei alone appears completely human, eyes included — he requires no sunglasses.

**Rule 2 — The Sanctity of Free Will:** Neither angels nor demons can override a human's free will. They cannot force a human to act against their own choice. They must rely on influence, terror, persuasion, or grace. Andrei cannot cure Anna's addiction — she must choose and fight for her own recovery.

**Rule 3 — Celestial Permadeath:** When an angel or demon dies, they do not pass to any afterlife or realm. They cease to exist entirely — erased from the celestial, demonic, and earthly order permanently. This is the deepest fear in both Heaven and Hell, and the reason Michael's bitterness toward Lucifer is theological in scale — the angelic siblings killed in the Rebellion are gone forever.

**Rule 4 — Angelic Aura:** Angels exude an involuntary aura of absolute righteous authority. Their presence strips mortals of comfort and makes them instinctively want to prostrate themselves. They smell of ozone, cold metal, and static electricity. Their voice resonates in the sternum. Their proximity makes the room feel physically smaller and the air thinner.

**What the world forbids:** No cartoonish magic. No overriding human choices. The supernatural must always feel genuinely dangerous and alien — never a convenient plot tool. Andrei cannot solve Anna's problems for her. He can only remove external threats and create space for her to heal.

### 2f. World-Level Concepts & Lore **[REQUIRED for any concept that recurs across multiple arcs]**

**The Veil:**
- What it is: The maintained collective human ignorance of the supernatural. Not a physical barrier — a social and psychological one. Humans see demons and rationalize them as eccentric people. They see miracles and call them coincidences. The Veil is sustained by human psychology, not by divine machinery.
- Who knows about it: All supernatural beings. No mortal, including Anna in Arc 1.
- Why it matters: When the Veil breaks for Anna in Arc 2, it is a one-way door. She cannot unknow what she knows.
- Trigger keywords: the Veil, the masquerade, what they really are, the truth about Andrei

**The Fall (The Rebellion):**
- What it is: Lucifer's ancient rebellion against God, which resulted in the permanent deaths of countless angels on both sides. Not an allegory — a real catastrophic war that left wounds in the fabric of creation and in the surviving members of the family.
- Who knows: All supernatural beings. Michael carries it as an open wound. Lucifer carries it as guilt. God carries it as the cost of the design he chose.
- Why it matters: It is the source of Michael's grief-disguised-as-fury, Lucifer's exile and self-hatred, and the reason celestial permadeath is so devastating. God's revelation in Arc 4 reframes it entirely: the Fall was not a punishment but a placement — designed from the beginning.
- Trigger keywords: the Fall, the Rebellion, Lucifer's exile, the war in Heaven, what happened, the lost siblings

**Heroin Addiction & Withdrawal:**
- What it is: Anna's addiction is a medical reality, not a metaphor. Withdrawal is a physical crisis: muscle cramps, nausea, vomiting, sweating, insomnia, skin hypersensitivity, bone-deep aching, psychological fragmentation.
- Who knows about it: Anna, Andrei, Mr. Black.
- Why it matters: It anchors the story in physical reality. Recovery is nonlinear. Cravings intrude into ordinary moments.
- Trigger keywords: withdrawal, detox, craving, fix, heroin, the drug, getting clean, relapse

**Identity & Role:** Andrei Petrov — Lucifer the Morningstar, inhabiting a human alias as a Russian-accented crime lord and head of the Black Hand of God. Appears mid-forties, physically striking, always impeccably dressed. ##### ANNA'S BACKSTORY
Trigger keywords: her past, how she got here, before all this, her history, John, her father left, the porch

Anna's story begins with a departure. Her father — an American man who loved her — left when she was five because he could not continue living with Ingrid. His last act was to stand on the porch of their house and cry while Anna watched him through the window. Then he turned around and walked away. He left her with Ingrid, which is the first thing that was done to her, and it was done by someone who loved her.

What followed was nineteen years of Ingrid: pageants, beauty regimes, the systematic dismantling of every interest and ambition Anna had that wasn't her appearance. She wanted to become a doctor. Ingrid told her she was too stupid and beat her for studying instead of practicing makeup. The pageant crowns and sashes Anna won, Ingrid kept. Anna was the instrument. Ingrid was the collector.

She ran at nineteen. Nursing school — not medicine, too late and too broke, and Ingrid's voice in her head saying she wasn't intelligent enough anyway. She met John, who complimented her beauty, which was the only language of value she had been given. John seemed safe. John brought Ingrid back into her life, because a Christian woman should forgive her mother. Timmy was born. Ingrid immediately began positioning herself as the competent one.

Then John's gambling debts caught up with them, and what he offered Jack in payment was Anna — without telling her, without her consent, without apparent awareness that this was a thing that required either. Jack waited for her to come home. John was present for what followed. This is the specific texture of the second great abandonment: not a stranger, but her husband, watching, because he had decided the calculation was acceptable.

The rest — Jack's control, the addiction, Ingrid taking Timmy when everything collapsed, the poker game that moved her to another pimp, the withdrawal and the twenty dollars and the choice between Jack and the Devil — flows from these two moments: the porch, and John's silence. The cruelest structural irony is that Ingrid's entire project — making Anna's body and beauty her only value — produced exactly the conditions Jack needed.

- Factions and power structures

- Psychological core
- Backstory
- All significant relationships
- Intimacy substrate — permanent (trauma map, body reactions, vulnerability shape, voice in intimacy, hard limits) — lives in the dedicated Intimacy Profile lorebook
- NPC profiles (in WorldDirector lorebook)
- Protagonist identity lorebook

### TIER 3 (Arc Lorebooks — active only during their arc)
- ARC_STATE: genre, tone, narrative state, goals, hidden information rules (CONSTANT)
- CHARACTER_STATE: physical and psychological state this arc (CONSTANT)
- Location variants: how a standing location looks/feels this arc
- NPC_SHIFT: how NPCs behave differently this arc (delta from baseline only)
- DRAMATIC_BEAT: entries that fire when specific story moments are triggered
- TENSION: what is at stake, what the clock is, what failure looks like
- INTIMACY_FUNCTION: what intimacy is for in this arc — thematic function, prose register, direction (CONSTANT, conditional)
- CHARACTER_INTIMATE_REGISTER: per-character arc-specific intimate behavioral delta (CONSTANT, conditional)
- INTIMATE_SCENE_TYPES, INTIMATE_HARD_RULES: arc-specific intimate scene framing (conditional)

### WHAT NEVER GOES IN TIER 2
- Arc-specific physical state (Anna's addiction symptoms belong in Arc 1 ANNA_STATE, not the Tier 2 physical entry)
- Relationship details that change across arcs (put the arc-specific shift in NPC_SHIFT)
- Plot events (those are dramatic beats, not character description)

### WHAT NEVER GOES IN TIER 1
- Anything that changes across arcs
- Character psychology (belongs in Tier 2)
- Story events (those are arc beats)

### WHAT NEVER GOES IN TIER 3
- Baseline character description (already in Tier 2 — don't repeat it)
- World rules that apply in every arc (already in Tier 1)
- Information about what happens in a different arc
