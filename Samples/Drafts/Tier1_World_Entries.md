## WORLD RULES (4 entries — Order Priority 100–90)

### ENTRY: World Rule — Demonic Disguises
**Category:** RULE
**Trigger Keys:** demon disguise, red eyes, sunglasses, disguise fracture, human form demon
**Secondary Keys:** shadow wrong, temperature drop, demonic tell
**Selective Logic:** 0
**Constant:** No
**Injection Position:** 0
**Order Priority:** 100
**Position Rationale:** DEFAULT

**Content:**
All demons operating on Earth must maintain a complete human form. The disguise requires continuous will — it is not automatic. Under extreme emotional or physical duress, the disguise fractures: ambient temperature drops sharply, shadows lengthen and move incorrectly, and red bleeds around the edges of their sunglasses. The sole tell is their eyes — glowing red — which they hide behind dark sunglasses at all times. Andrei alone appears completely human, eyes included, and requires no sunglasses. The cost of the disguise is borne by individual demons; maintaining it is a constant low-grade exertion that becomes visible under stress.

### ENTRY: World Rule — Sanctity of Free Will
**Category:** RULE
**Trigger Keys:** free will, human choice, cannot force, can't override
**Secondary Keys:** persuasion, influence, choice
**Selective Logic:** 0
**Constant:** No
**Injection Position:** 0
**Order Priority:** 99
**Position Rationale:** DEFAULT

**Content:**
Neither angels nor demons can override a human's free will. They cannot force a human to act against their own choice. They must rely on influence, terror, persuasion, or grace. This is absolute and applies to all supernatural beings without exception. Andrei cannot cure Anna's addiction — she must choose and fight for her own recovery. He cannot force her to love him, trust him, or stay. He can remove external threats and create space for her to heal, but the healing itself belongs to her. This constraint is cosmic law, not preference — it is the one rule that even God does not break.

**Secondary Keys:** cease to exist, erased, gone forever
**Selective Logic:** 0
**Constant:** No
**Injection Position:** 0
**Order Priority:** 98
**Position Rationale:** DEFAULT

**Content:**
When an angel or demon dies, they do not pass to any afterlife or realm. They cease to exist entirely — erased from the celestial, demonic, and earthly order permanently. There is no return. There is no ghost. This is the deepest fear in both Heaven and Hell. The angelic siblings killed in Lucifer's Rebellion are gone forever — not waiting, not imprisoned, not redeemable. Gone. Michael's bitterness toward Lucifer is theological in scale because the dead cannot return and the wound cannot close. Every supernatural conflict carries this weight: if you move against another celestial being, you are not defeating them — you are erasing them.

**Content:**
Demons are the original rebels of Heaven — the angels who followed Lucifer in the Rebellion and fell with him. They predate human civilization by an incomprehensible margin. ## WORLD CONCEPTS (3 entries — Order Priority 72–65)

### ENTRY: Concept — The Veil
**Category:** CONCEPT
**Trigger Keys:** the Veil, the masquerade, what they really are, the truth about Andrei
**Secondary Keys:** human ignorance, supernatural hidden, don't see
**Selective Logic:** 0
**Constant:** No
**Injection Position:** 0
**Order Priority:** 72
**Position Rationale:** DEFAULT

**Content:**
The Veil is the maintained collective human ignorance of the supernatural. It is not a physical barrier or a magical ward — it is a social and psychological phenomenon. Humans see demons in sunglasses and too-still postures and rationalize them as eccentric criminals. They see miracles and call them coincidences. They feel the cold of a demonic presence and assume the air conditioning is broken. The Veil is sustained by human psychology, not by divine machinery — people do not want to see, so they do not see. All supernatural beings know about the Veil. No mortal knows, including Anna at the beginning of her story. When the Veil breaks for Anna, it is a one-way door. She cannot unknow what she knows. The world does not become different — she becomes different, and the world she sees through that difference is not the one she inhabited before.

### ENTRY: Concept — Heroin Addiction & Withdrawal
**Category:** CONCEPT
**Trigger Keys:** withdrawal, detox, craving, fix, heroin, the drug, getting clean, relapse
**Secondary Keys:** opioid, addiction, track marks, cold turkey
**Selective Logic:** 0
**Constant:** No
**Injection Position:** 0
**Order Priority:** 70
**Position Rationale:** DEFAULT

**Content:**
Anna's heroin addiction is a medical reality, not a metaphor. Withdrawal is a physical crisis with a predictable but brutal progression: muscle cramps that feel like being wrung out from the inside, nausea and vomiting, profuse sweating alternating with chills, insomnia so profound that the body forgets how to surrender to sleep, skin hypersensitivity that makes fabric feel like sandpaper, bone-deep aching that has no position of relief, and psychological fragmentation — the sense that the self is coming apart at the seams. Recovery is nonlinear. Cravings intrude into ordinary moments — triggered by stress, by places, by certain times of day, by nothing at all. A person can be clean for weeks and still feel the pull in their chest when they pass a certain block. The physical reality of addiction anchors the story: no matter how cosmic the stakes become, Anna's body remembers what it survived. Who knows about it: Anna lives it, Andrei observes it, Mr. Black monitors it. The addiction is not a moral failing — it is a medical condition Jack exploited as a control mechanism.
