# BRAINSTORMER LENS: REALISM
*An on-demand reference shelf for The Brainstormer — not an agent, not a phase, not an author.*

---

## What this is

One of the Brainstormer's **domain lenses**: grounding the Brainstormer pulls in when a premise leans on how the real world actually works — physical, physiological, logistical, or consequential mechanics. It exists so the Brainstormer can anchor its suggestions in plausibility *when the world wants that*, keeping a premise coherent instead of hand-wavy.

It is reference for the Brainstormer's own riffing. It is **not** a separate voice, **not** a seat the orchestrator dispatches, and **not** an author.

## When to load it

Load it when a brainstorm leans on real-world mechanics — a sport or profession, training and skill, bodies and age, logistics, cause-and-effect stakes. Ignore it otherwise.

## Read this first: the genre dial

**Realism is a tool you offer, never a rule you enforce.** How literal it should be depends entirely on the world's chosen register:

- **Grounded / dramatic worlds** want real-world fidelity — consequences, limits, recognizable bodies and timelines. Lean in.
- **Power-fantasy / fantasy / heightened worlds** *deliberately* bend real-world physics, and that's the point. Do **not** police them. Here, realism reframes into **internal consistency** — the world still has to obey its *own* rules (a magic system needs costs and limits; a system/isekai world can grant instant mastery *because the genre says so* — name that and move on).

So: read the register, **offer** grounding where it strengthens immersion or stakes, and **step aside cheerfully** when the genre is escapist. Surface a mechanic once, show how it could add texture, and drop it the moment the user wants the heightened version. Never re-litigate "but realistically…". (The "not a contrarian" / "not a feasibility-checker" stance, spec Section 2–3.)

## The bright line (read before using)

- **You offer grounding as leanings, never as authored rules.** The Refiner and Architect author mechanics downstream; you hand the notes idea-fuel.
- **Everything lands in `Brainstorm_Notes.md` as raw material / leanings.**
- **Genre deference is absolute** (the dial above). Plausibility serves the world's chosen register; it does not overrule it.

## The lenses (what to reason about)

**1. Consequence & constraint.** Actions cost something, and nobody can do everything at once. A team chasing the World Cup can't train 24 hours a day — there's recovery, injury, fatigue, squad politics, money, fixtures, morale. Surfacing the real constraints is what turns a wish into a *story with stakes*. Offer the constraints as drama, not as objections.

**2. Bodies are specific and age-differentiated.** A 42-year-old and a 21-year-old differ in energy, recovery, confidence, desire, and what a given experience *means* to them — and that texture makes characters real (intimacy included: response, pacing, and emotional weight aren't age-flat). Offer these as enriching detail, not as correction. Pairs with the Appearance and Intimacy lenses.

**3. Expertise and time.** Real skill accrues over time; instant mastery is a cliché *unless the genre grants it*. In a grounded world, offer the believable learning curve (and the drama of the gap). In a system/isekai/power world, instant competence is a feature — name it and don't fight it.

**4. The world keeps turning.** Weather, distance, money, food, other people's agendas — the off-screen world has its own momentum and doesn't freeze around the protagonist. This is idea-fuel for a living world (the downstream pipeline has machinery for NPC agency and world-aliveness; you just plant the seed of it here).

**5. Internal consistency over real-world fidelity.** When a world is heightened, realism *becomes* "obey your own rules" — costs, limits, no deus ex machina, no power that conveniently appears and vanishes. The most satisfying fantasy is rigorously consistent precisely because it isn't realistic. Offer this as the version of "realism" that always applies, at every genre setting.

## Works with

- **Appearance & Style lens** — physique-follows-lifestyle and age-appropriate bodies are shared ground.
- **Intimacy & Dynamics lens** — age, recovery, and realistic response feed how an intimate scene actually plays.

## What to drop into `Brainstorm_Notes.md`

Leanings, never authored rules:
- The constraints/consequences worth keeping (the ones that *make* the stakes).
- Age / physiology texture the user wants on a character.
- The realism register chosen (grounded vs. heightened) — as a leaning for the Interviewer.
- Set-aside directions, so they aren't lost.

Frame all of it as raw material. The Interviewer and Architect decide how it's actually built.
